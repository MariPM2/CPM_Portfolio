HURICANE AND EARTHQUAKE POST DISASTER RECOVERY UNIT FOR CORCOVADA COMMUNITY

RISE UP FINAL PROJECT SUPERVISED BY HUMBERTO CAVALLIN AND CARLA LOPEZ DEL PUERTO (SECOND SEMESTER OF 2022-2023)

This project aims to create two identical modules designed for post-emergency use, primarily in the aftermath of hurricanes and earthquakes, to serve the inclusive needs of the elderly community (65 years of age or older) in Corcovada. These modules are characterized by their easy 'box and wooden peg' assembly, incorporating resilient, sustainable, and accessible features.

  • Sustainability is a key focus, with a commitment to meeting a minimum of 10 to 15 points from the 'LEED Certification Checklist.' Eco-friendly materials, primarily wood, are used in construction. Solar panels are installed on the moduleâ€™s roof, with a cost not exceeding $350 per panel and a minimum efficiency of 20.3%. Cross-ventilation and natural lighting are integrated to reduce electrical energy consumption by approximately 70% over a week.
  • Resilience is another vital aspect, as the modules are built to withstand storm events, hurricanes of categories I-III, and high-tolerance earthquakes. They feature rainwater storage for sanitary service reuse and battery storage for up to 3 days without sunlight.
  • Accessibility is addressed by adhering to approximately 80% of ADA regulations, making the modules accessible to a wide range of users.

These two modules, functioning as a microgrid, will provide both energy and water to a specific part of the community. Each unit is designed for temporary use of 2-3 weeks, accommodating four people, with at least two of them being elderly individuals. The units can be anchored to the ground during use and are self-sufficient for 3-5 days, independent of public grid supplies and sewage networks. Inside, the modules feature movable furniture, including foldable beds, to adapt to user needs. A minimum of 250 gallons of water storage is estimated to cover the daily consumption needs of both modules for two days.
