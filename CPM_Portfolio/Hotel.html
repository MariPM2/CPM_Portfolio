Seashore Hotel: Where Hospitality Meets Ocean-Inspired Organic Design

In my last architectural studio design course, I had the unique opportunity to work on the Seashore Hotel project, a captivating class assignment that challenged me to blend creativity with practical architectural principles. The architectural studio course I undertook was a transformative educational experience, purposefully designed to equip me with the knowledge and skills needed to craft complete architectural plans while integrating the latest technology and emphasizing sustainable design and construction techniques.

My primary goal for the Seashore Hotel project was to seamlessly integrate the hotel with its awe-inspiring beachfront location, harmonizing the design with the natural surroundings. The strategic positioning of the hotel aimed to maximize panoramic views and harness the abundant natural light. I designed the exterior of the building with a carefully selected palette of natural materials, including stone, wood, and glass, which beautifully echoed the coastal elements, adding an element of elegance and timelessness to the design. Inside the hotel, I devoted meticulous attention to designing interior spaces that went beyond functionality, aiming to provide guests with the utmost comfort and tranquility. Inclusivity was a core consideration, as I specially designed two rooms to meet ADA specifications, ensuring accessibility for all visitors. This project was a testament to my capabilities of architectural creativity and thoughtful design. Although it was a class project, it offered an immersive experience that captivated and embraced the beauty of the surrounding environment, ultimately creating a truly unique and inviting space that left a lasting impression on all who explored my vision. This project embodied the holistic and forward-thinking approach to architectural design that the course instilled in me, bringing together all the elements I learned into a tangible and impactful creation.
