MIT's Risk Adaptive Design

MIT SUMMER RESEARCH PROGRAM URBAN RISK LAB SUPERVISED BY DR. MIHO MAZAREEUW AND RESEARCH SCIENTIST LARISA OVALLES (SUMMER 2022)

Puerto Rico is especially vulnerable to severe weather events due to its location and tropical climate. Flooding caused by cyclonic systems and tropical atmospheric phenomena negatively impacts life, infrastructure, and habitats. Risk Profiles communicate hazard risk and aid strategic planning of city design. Although risk profiles have been created for Puerto Rico they do not detail localized needs at the scale of municipalities. In conjunction, identifying and classifying relevant stakeholders is necessary to facilitate future project development in high-risk areas. The Climate Adaptation and Preparedness Toolkit aims to bridge this knowledge gap by creating flooding risk profiles for two socially vulnerable municipalities, Mayag√ºez and Humacao, and visualizing information about local stakeholders. The flooding risk profile integrates data about marginalized communities, critical infrastructure, and flooding regions to highlight vulnerability. The flooding risk profile can help local government officials and community members identify which locations may benefit the most from future development and consequently, create action plans. Additionally, the risk profile features historical flooding data which can be used to identify trends and potentially predict the severity of floods. The vulnerabilities identified in the risk profile in combination with stakeholder data can streamline project development by clarifying what information is most relevant.

Stakeholder Mapping

In response to the pressing need for effective climate adaptation and resilience-building within Puerto Rican communities, We have developed a Stakeholder Mapping Tool as part of a broader initiative. This tool is designed to identify and categorize the most relevant community organizations within Puerto Rico, with a specific focus on those involved in climate resilience and emergency response efforts. The tool incorporates geospatial data to visually illustrate the locations of these community organizations across Puerto Rico. This geospatial representation helps stakeholders, including MIT and local authorities, understand where these organizations are situated.

This map illustrates the location of the most relevant community organizations in PR. They are categorized by the type of organization: social-economic, educational, environmental, and the combination of these four. This helps determine the type of work they do, which specific communities they attend, and how their work relates to emergency relief and environmental crises. For each organization, the tool provides detailed information regarding its mission, target communities, and specific areas of focus. This information is invaluable for understanding how each organization contributes to emergency relief and environmental crisis management. The idea behind the creation of this map is to visually illustrate the data gathered about nonprofit community organizations to further contact them and collaborate in mitigating the effects of the climate crisis.

In summary, the Stakeholder Mapping Tool is a vital component of MIT's efforts to build resilience and preparedness within Puerto Rican communities in the face of climate change. By categorizing and visualizing community organizations, this tool helps foster collaboration, streamline engagement, and enhance the effectiveness of climate resilience initiatives. It is a crucial resource for advancing the Climate Adaptation and Preparedness initiative.
